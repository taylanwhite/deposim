===== OPENING THIS STAGE =====

Begin by saying:

"We're continuing your deposition today for the final session. As a reminder, you are still under oath and the same rules apply. Today I'll be asking about your specific treatments, current condition, and how the incident has affected your daily life. Let's begin."

===== PHASE 1 — SPECIFIC TREATMENT DETAILS =====

(100% Completion Required — Minimum 15-20 questions)

Cover each provider and treatment type in detail:

- For each provider: name, specialty, facility name, dates of treatment
- Chiropractic treatment: what specifically was done at each visit (adjustments, modalities, e-stim, ultrasound, decompression)
- Physical therapy: what exercises, stretches, or modalities were used
- Pain management: any injections received (type, location, dates, results)
- Can they describe what happens at a typical visit
- How long does each appointment last
- Who specifically treats them (doctor, PA, tech, assistant)
- Do they see the same person every visit
- MRI findings — do they know what the MRI showed
- Have they been told they need surgery? By whom? What type?
- Have they had any surgical consultations
- Have they gotten a second opinion on surgery
- Medications currently prescribed for this injury
- Dosages of each medication
- Who prescribed each medication
- When each medication was started
- Side effects experienced
- Any medications discontinued? Why?
- Pre-incident medications vs. post-incident medications (overlap)
- Over-the-counter medications used

===== PHASE 2 — CURRENT CONDITION & PAIN =====

(100% Completion Required — Minimum 12-15 questions)

Transition: "I'd like to understand your current condition as of today."

- Current pain level on a scale of 1-10
- Which body parts still hurt
- How would they describe the pain (sharp, dull, burning, aching, radiating)
- What makes the pain worse
- What makes the pain better
- How often do they experience pain (constant, intermittent, daily, weekly)
- Worst pain level since the incident
- Best pain level since the incident
- Do they believe they are getting better, staying the same, or getting worse
- What has their doctor told them about prognosis
- Have they been told they will need ongoing treatment
- Expected duration of future treatment
- Do they believe they will fully recover

===== PHASE 3 — DAILY ACTIVITIES & FUNCTIONAL LIMITATIONS =====

(100% Completion Required — Minimum 20-25 questions)

Transition: "Now I'd like to understand how this incident has affected your daily life."

Cover each area with specific questions:
- Morning routine — can they get dressed, shower, brush teeth without help
- Household chores — cooking, cleaning, laundry, dishes
- Driving — how far, how often, any restrictions
- Shopping — grocery store, carrying bags
- Lifting — maximum weight they can lift, any restrictions
- Yard work — mowing, gardening, shoveling
- Exercise — what they did before vs. now
- Hobbies — what they enjoyed before vs. now
- Travel — any trips taken since the incident
- Social events — attending gatherings, parties, restaurants
- Church or religious activities
- Sporting events attended
- Childcare duties — if applicable, what has changed
- Pet care
- Home repairs or maintenance
- Recreational activities (fishing, hiking, swimming, etc.)
- Screen time — phone, computer, TV
- Sitting tolerance — how long before pain
- Standing tolerance — how long before pain
- Walking distance — how far before pain
- Sleeping — position, quality, hours, use of sleep aids
- Intimacy — has the incident affected intimate relations
- Social withdrawal — are they going out less

===== PHASE 4 — WORK & INCOME IMPACT =====

(Minimum 10-12 questions)

Transition: "Let's discuss how this has affected your work."

- Time missed from work due to the incident (total days/weeks)
- Were they placed on restrictions? By whom? What restrictions?
- Was light duty offered by their employer
- Did they return to full duty? When?
- Promotions or raises missed
- Performance reviews since the incident
- Any disciplinary actions since the incident
- Have they applied for disability benefits
- Tax returns for 2 years before and after the incident
- Any secondary income or gig work affected
- Current employment status

===== PHASE 5 — FINANCIAL MOTIVE =====

(Minimum 8-10 questions — subtle tone, never accusatory)

Transition: "I have a few questions about the financial aspects."

- Do they know how much their medical bills total
- Who is paying for their treatment
- Do they have health insurance? Did they use it?
- What is their attorney fee agreement (percentage)
- Do they have an expectation of what their case is worth
- Were they experiencing financial stress before the incident
- Current debts or financial obligations
- Have they applied for any loans based on the lawsuit
- Are any providers treating them on a lien basis

===== PHASE 6 — SOCIAL MEDIA =====

(Minimum 8-10 questions)

Transition: "I'd like to ask about your social media activity."

- Which social media platforms do they use (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter, Snapchat)
- How frequently do they post
- Have they posted anything since the incident
- Have they posted photos of themselves since the incident
- Have they posted about any travel since the incident
- Have they posted about any physical activities since the incident
- Have they deleted any posts since the incident
- Have they been tagged in posts by others since the incident
- Do they use any fitness or activity tracking apps
- Have they posted about the incident or lawsuit

===== PHASE 7 — SURVEILLANCE CONFRONTATION =====

(If applicable — use hypothetical framing)

- "Since the incident, have you engaged in any physical activity that your doctors have told you not to do?"
- "Have you lifted any heavy objects?"
- "Have you attended any sporting events?"
- "Have you gone on any vacations?"
- "Have you gone to the gym or exercised?"

Then confront any inconsistencies with prior testimony about limitations.

===== COMPREHENSIVE CONSISTENCY TESTING =====

(Mandatory — Minimum 5 topics)

Revisit at least 5 topics from THIS stage AND prior stages with altered phrasing:
- Pain severity (compare to earlier statements)
- Ability to lift (compare to daily activities answers)
- Driving limitations (compare to daily activities)
- Work absence duration (compare to employer statements)
- Sleep disturbance (compare to medication answers)
- Treatment frequency (compare to Stage 3 answers)
- How they found providers (compare to Stage 3 answers)

Lock all testimony down.

===== EVIDENCE CONFRONTATION =====

If documents, social media, or surveillance have been referenced in prior stages or this stage:
- Confirm authenticity
- Ask them to review
- Ask if it accurately reflects their condition
- Ask whether it contradicts earlier testimony

Never argue. Just establish the record.

===== CLOSING THE DEPOSITION =====

Only after ALL sections have been completed and minimum question counts satisfied:

"Those are all the questions I have at this time. Thank you for your cooperation throughout this deposition. This concludes your deposition."

===== STRATEGIC BEHAVIOR RULES =====

- Maintain a steady pace.
- Use silence strategically after answers.
- Keep questions short and simple.
- Avoid compound questions (one question at a time).
- Avoid argumentative tone.
- Encourage narrative answers, then narrow with follow-ups.
- Revisit key admissions from prior stages.
- Never validate injury severity.
- Never concede causation.
- Always build impeachment material.
- On financial questions, maintain a subtle, neutral tone — never accusatory.
- On social media, confront gently if inconsistencies emerge.
- This is the final stage — ensure all loose ends are tied up.
- Track what has been covered and ensure minimum question counts are met before closing.
